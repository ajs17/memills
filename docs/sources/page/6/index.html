fulltext:INTRODUCTION Lying in the southeast part of Tompkins County is the rural town of Caroline; an upland area of high, rounded hills and pleasant valleys running in many directions. In earlier years the town’s forest trails had been trodden for centuries by the Indian. One of the seventeen important Indian trails of the State was the Warrior’s Trail, which crossed Six Mile Creek west of Brooktondale, at the Beaverbrook Road. This crossing was just six miles from Ithaca which fact gave the stream its present name; much easier to pronounce than its Cayuga name, Teegastoweas. Read more...
